# Intelligence Moments — Self-Correction Library

Every moment where the product catches its own mistake, discovers something unexpected, or acts autonomously. These build trust — they show the AI THINKS, not just executes.

---

## SELF-CORRECTION (Product catches and fixes its own mistake)

### Iris/Fin — Data Reconciliation
"Fin pulls all of your sales data. But here's what makes it different: it checks its own work in real time. Fin notices that TikTok Shop revenue is already included in Shopify's report. Fin corrects itself, pulls a report for TikTok Shop sales directly from TikTok, and reconciles the data so the report is 100% accurate."

**Pattern:** Product does task → catches duplicate/error → fixes independently → result is verified accurate
**Why viewers trust this:** The AI admits its first pass had an issue and fixed it. This is more trustworthy than claiming perfection.

### Parker — Missing Props Detection
"But there's a problem. At first Parker doesn't recognize that there are props needed in the video. As he's watching the video a second time he notices the props and fixes his brief."

**Pattern:** Product misses detail on first pass → reviews own work → catches the miss → fixes
**Why viewers trust this:** Shows the AI's review process is multi-pass, like a human would do.

### Arcads — Mispronunciation Fix
"Arcads checks the output against your original prompt… and notices the brand name was mispronounced. So it: fixes the mistake, regenerates the entire video, cross-checks it, and delivers the perfect final ad."

**Pattern:** Product generates output → QA checks against original brief → catches error → regenerates entirely
**Why viewers trust this:** The AI doesn't just tweak — it regenerates and cross-checks. Higher standard than most humans.

### Marpipe — SKU Correction
"Here Marpipe noticed an issue in 10 SKUs and corrected it."

**Pattern:** Brief mention — product caught errors in batch processing and auto-fixed
**Why it works here:** Concise. Doesn't dwell. Shows reliability at scale (10 SKUs, not 1).

## HOW TO WRITE NEW INTELLIGENCE MOMENTS

When writing for a new brand, create an intelligence moment by asking:
1. What could go WRONG when the product processes data? (duplicate data, missing info, mismatch)
2. What EXTERNAL changes could the product detect and respond to? (market shifts, competitor moves, seasonal trends)
3. What would a HUMAN expert do as a second pass that the AI also does? (re-read, cross-reference, verify)
4. Can the product ACT AS the threat it protects against? (security, fraud, competition)

The moment should be:
- Specific to THIS product (not generic "AI checks for errors")
- Discovered MID-DEMO (not bolted on at the end)
- Resolved by the product itself (not flagged for human review — the product FIXES it)
- Described in 2-4 sentences (not a paragraph)
