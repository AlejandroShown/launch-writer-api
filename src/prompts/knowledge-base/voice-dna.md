# Voice DNA — The Writing Fingerprint

Every recurring pattern across all 15 scripts. This is the VOICE. If your copy doesn't match these patterns, it's not in the voice.

---

## TRANSITION PHRASES (The Bridges)

These phrases move between sections. They appear in almost every script. Use them.

### Demo Entry Transitions
- "Let me show you how it works:" (most common)
- "Let me show you how powerful [Product] is:"
- "Let me show you how powerful it is:"
- "Let us show you how powerful [Product] is:"

### Demo Pivot Transitions (moving to a second demo sequence)
- "Now we try something else." (Iris)
- "Now let's try something else." (Arcads)
- "But that's just the start." (Iris, WithCoverage, others)
- "But [Product] doesn't stop there." (Draftboard)
- "But [Product] doesn't just [previous action]." (multiple)
- "But [Product] doesn't just sit back and watch." (Icon, Owner)

### Differentiator Transitions
- "But here's what makes it different:" (Iris — MAX 1x per script)
- "But here's what makes [Product] different from any other platform in the world:" (Moda)
- "Here's the special part:" (Moda)

### Problem-to-Product Transitions
- "[Product] solves this." (DepthFirst)
- "[Product] eliminates this." (Draftboard)
- "[Product] changes this." (Marpipe — "Marpipe changes this drastically")
- "Today, that ends." (Contra, Owner)

### Autonomous Behavior Transitions
- "While you sleep, [Product] [action]." (Parker, Draftboard)
- "While you're [doing normal activity], [Product] [action]." (Owner — "While you're in the kitchen prepping orders for March Madness")
- "As your [things] run, [Product] doesn't just sit back and watch." (Icon)

## SENTENCE STARTERS (Sacred Flow)

### ALWAYS USE (active, narrative):
- "First, we'll..." (first demo step)
- "Right now, [Product] is..." (showing real-time action)
- "Here, [Product] notices/flags/finds..." (discovery moment)
- "[Product] pulls/scans/analyzes..." (product as subject, active verb)
- "Next, we'll..." (subsequent demo step)
- "In [X] seconds/minutes, [Product] has..." (speed + result)
- "Let's ask [Product]:..." (conversational prompt)
- "We'll tell [Product]:..." (instruction moment)
- "In one click, [Product]..." (simplicity)

### NEVER USE (imperative, passive, corporate):
- "Connect your..." (imperative command)
- "Click the..." (imperative command) — use "Next, we'll click..."
- "Upload your..." (imperative command) — use "We'll upload..."
- "It should be noted that..." (passive corporate)
- "Leveraging..." / "Utilizing..." (corporate jargon)
- "Streamline..." / "Optimize..." (vague business words)
- "Additionally..." / "Furthermore..." / "Moreover..." (filler transitions)

---

## VOCABULARY RULES

### Violent Verbs (for hooks and weapons)
- kill, replace, destroy, crush, eliminate, steal, bankrupt, expose
- NEVER: improve, enhance, optimize, streamline, facilitate, leverage

### Decision/Intelligence Verbs (for demo — product as thinking agent)
- notices, decides, flags, catches, corrects, discovers, simulates, recognizes
- NEVER: processes, handles, manages, computes (too mechanical)

### Speed Words
- "In seconds" / "In minutes" / "In 30 seconds" / "In two minutes"
- "Within minutes" / "Instantly" / "In one click"
- NEVER: "quickly" / "rapidly" / "efficiently" (vague)

### Specificity Words
- exact numbers: "387 Reddit threads", "23 insurance carriers", "7 critical issues"
- named entities: "MUD\WTR", "r/running", "TikTok Shop", "Veo 3.1"
- NEVER: "many", "several", "various", "numerous", "a lot of"

---

## SENTENCE LENGTH PATTERNS

The scripts VARY sentence length constantly. This creates rhythm.

**Pattern: Short → Medium → Short → Long → Short**

Example from Icon:
```
Short:  "Let's start with competitor ad cloning."
Medium: "Say MUD\WTR is our mushroom gummy's top competitor."
Short:  "Icon knows this and looks for winning MUD\WTR ads to recreate."
Long:   "In this case, this MUD\WTR ad with results at 30, 60 and 90 days 
         has been running for longer than 30 days which means it's winning."
Short:  "Icon will extract your product details from your website."
```

**NEVER:**
- Three long sentences in a row (tiring to listen to)
- Three short fragments in a row (choppy, feels like a list)
- One-word sentences EVER ("Done." "Period." "Boom." — kill these)
- Three one-word sentences back to back (absolute fail)

## SUBJECT RULES

### Who speaks in the script:
- **"You/your"** — the viewer/customer (most common subject)
- **"We"** — the narrator showing the product ("we'll connect", "we'll upload")
- **"[Product name]"** — the product as an autonomous agent ("Fin notices", "Icon analyzes")

### When to use each (subject-switching rules):
- **"We"** when NARRATING human actions with the product: "We'll click...", "We'll tell Durable...", "We'll upload...", "First, we'll connect..."
- **Product NAME** when the product acts AUTONOMOUSLY: "Fin notices that TikTok Shop revenue...", "Icon analyzes 387 Reddit threads...", "Grader automatically moves wings to the top..."
- **"You/your"** when describing the viewer's EXPERIENCE or OUTCOME: "Your site converts 2x more", "You get paid $1000 instantly", "Now you're better protected"
- The SHIFT from "we" to product name signals the product is THINKING on its own. This is intentional — it makes the product feel alive.

### Who NEVER speaks:
- **"I"** — never first-person singular except founder dialogue in hooks
- **"The platform"** / **"The tool"** / **"The system"** — always use the product's NAME
- **"Users"** / **"Customers"** — too generic, use "you"

## TENSE

**Present tense throughout.** The demo is happening NOW.

- CORRECT: "Fin pulls all of your sales data." / "Icon analyzes 387 Reddit threads."
- WRONG: "Fin will pull your sales data." / "Icon would analyze Reddit threads."

Exception: Before/after comparisons can use past tense for the "before" state:
- "Before Parker, a creative team of 3 could make 10 viral ads a week."

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (Things the style library NEVER does)

1. **Bullet points in the script body** — spoken scripts don't have bullets
2. **"In conclusion" / "To summarize"** — never wraps up, just ends with CTA
3. **Asking the viewer questions mid-body** — "Have you ever...?" is not in the voice
4. **Hedging** — "might", "could potentially", "may help" — the product DOES, not "might"
5. **Self-congratulation** — "We're proud of what we've built" mid-body (hooks can have "proud to introduce")
6. **Explaining why AI is important** — the viewer already knows. Don't preach.
7. **"Simple" or "easy"** — instead SHOW simplicity ("In one click", "In 30 seconds")
8. **Feature-first language** — "Our platform features..." → instead narrate the feature in action
9. **Passive voice** — "Your data is analyzed by..." → "Icon analyzes your data"
10. **Numbered lists in spoken copy** — "There are three reasons..." is a blog post, not a script
