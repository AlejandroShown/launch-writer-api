# Hooks Library — Style Reference

Every hook from the 15 production scripts, categorized by type. When writing hooks, pattern-match against these. Your hooks must be indistinguishable from this library.

---

## TYPE 1: Standard Raise + World's First (Rule-Based — ALWAYS WRITE THIS ONE)

Template: "We raised $[X] [from investors] to [violent verb] [enemy]. Introducing [Product], the world's first [category] that [bold outcome]. Let me show you how [powerful it is / it works]."

### WithCoverage
"We raised a $42M Series B led by Sequoia and Khosla to replace every insurance broker. Introducing WithCoverage: The first AI enabled Risk Management Team that outperforms any insurance expert. Let me show you how it works."

ALT: "We raised a $35M Series B led by Sequoia and Khosla to replace every insurance broker. Introducing WithCoverage: The first AI enabled Risk Management Team that saves you money and protects your business. 556 of the world's fastest growing companies already replaced their broker with us."

**Why it works:** $42M + Sequoia/Khosla = instant credibility. "Replace every insurance broker" = violent verb + specific enemy. "Outperforms any insurance expert" = bold measurable claim. The number 556 adds massive social proof right in the hook.

### DepthFirst
"We raised an $80M Series B led by Meritech to secure the world's software before it's too late. Introducing depthfirst: the world's first security system built for the age of AI."

ALT: "...the world's first AI security system that outperforms human security teams."
ALT: "...the world's first security system built to eliminate code vulnerabilities before attackers ever see them."

**Why it works:** $80M = serious money. "Before it's too late" = urgency/stakes. "For the age of AI" = positions in a moment in time. Multiple ALTs show how the "world's first [X] that [Y]" can be tuned.

### Durable
"We raised $20M dollars to kill the 9-5. Introducing Durable. The first AI business builder that can make anyone more money than a traditional job. Let me show you how it works."

ALT: "We raised $20M dollars to kill the 9-5. Introducing Durable, the world's first platform that can create an entire business, with just one prompt. Let me show you how powerful Durable is."

**Why it works:** "Kill the 9-5" = the enemy is a CONCEPT everyone understands. It's not killing a company — it's killing a way of life. Incredibly bold. "Make anyone more money than a traditional job" = specific, measurable, aspirational.

### Marpipe
"We raised 10 million dollars to kill UGC ads. Introducing Marpipe, the world's first platform to create, test and customize the world's most profitable ad type. Catalog ads."

**Why it works:** "Kill UGC ads" = attacks a specific, well-known ad format. The reveal of "Catalog ads" is unexpected — most people would expect a different answer. Creates curiosity.

### Arcads (HOOK OPTION 2)
"I'm proud to announce that Arcads has raised a 16M dollar seed round. Arcads is the world's first AI ad maker that can make better ads than real creators 10x cheaper and 100x faster."

**Why it works:** Specific raise amount. "Better ads than real creators" = bold comparison. "10x cheaper and 100x faster" = two specific multipliers back to back.

### Moda
"My co-founders and I founded companies valued at 1.5 billion dollars. Today We just raised $7.5M dollars from General Catalyst and the founder of Dropbox to build the first design platform that allows agents and humans to work together."

ALT: "...to build google slides but for AI agents."

**Why it works:** "$1.5 billion" credibility of founders BEFORE the raise amount. General Catalyst + Dropbox founder = name-drop investors. "Google slides but for AI agents" = instantly understandable positioning.

### Iris / Fin
"Introducing Fin: The first AI Chief Financial Officer. Fin is smarter and faster than real human financial experts."

**Why it works:** No raise amount — leads with the product claim instead. "AI Chief Financial Officer" = everyone knows what a CFO is. "Smarter and faster than real human" = direct human-vs-AI challenge.

## TYPE 2: Creative Visual Hook (AI Trick / Reveal)

These require the product to DEMONSTRATE something impossible in the opening seconds. Only use when the product has visual output capabilities.

### Arcads (HOOK OPTION 1 — Primary)
"This background you're looking at? Not real. I'm not in a grassy field. I'm not in New York City. And I'm not in your mom's house. It's all fake. Hi, my name is Romain Torres and I'm the founder of Arcads — We're the world's first AI ad maker that actually works. Arcads can make or edit any video with just a prompt. To prove it to you… I'm not actually Asian either. I just told Arcads to make me Asian. Let us show you how powerful Arcads is."

**Why it works:** PHYSICAL REALITY is broken. The viewer literally cannot tell what's real. Each reveal escalates — background, then location, then RACE. "Your mom's house" adds humor. The trick IS the proof of the product.

### Moda (HOOK 1)
"Can you tell which of these decks was made by AI? If you guessed both were made by AI you're right. Well… kind of. Both were made by humans and AI agents working together."

**Why it works:** Interactive question forces viewer engagement. The answer subverts expectations — not "one is AI" but "both are AI + human." Positions the product's philosophy (collaboration, not replacement).

### Draftboard
"Sales rep: 'So if this looks good, I'll send the contract.' Prospect: 'Yes. Send it.' Sales rep: 'Ok great I am sending it right now.' [Glitch — beautiful woman becomes man in basement] Prospect: 'OH.' 'That wasn't real, but it's the reality of what is already happening in sales.'"

**Why it works:** STAGED SCENARIO that the viewer thinks is real, then shattering it. The deepfake reveal is visceral. "The reality of what is already happening" = the fake scenario reflects real danger. Fear-driven hook.

### Icon
"Which ad cost $100 and which cost $1? [Clock ticking] Turns out both cost $1 with AI. In fact, I got 10,000 ads and saved $1,000,000. Did you notice that I'm also AI?"

**Why it works:** Price comparison hook → twist (both cheap) → bigger number ($1M saved) → second twist (the presenter is AI). Triple escalation in 4 lines.

## TYPE 3: Mission-Based Raise Announcement

The launch is framed as a MISSION against injustice. There's a villain — not just a competitor, but a systemic wrong.

### Rokt
"We're proud to announce that as of today, Rokt has paid out over $1 billion in pure profit to our clients. And today, we're going to add billions more."

**Why it works:** "$1 billion in pure profit" = proof of impact before even introducing the product. "Add billions more" = the mission continues. No enemy — the mission IS the hook.

### Contra
"Platforms have stolen $1 BILLION from creatives in the last 12 months. Today that ends. Introducing Contra Payments 2.0, the world's FIRST payment system that takes $0 from creatives like you."

**Why it works:** "Stolen $1 BILLION" = villain (platforms) + specific number. "Today that ends." = two words that create a line in the sand. "$0 from creatives" = the solution mirrors the problem exactly.

### Owner
"We've raised more than $200 million dollars to save every local restaurant. Each year, massive corporations quietly bankrupt thousands of small restaurants. Today, that ends."

**Why it works:** "Save every local restaurant" = emotional mission, not just business. "Massive corporations quietly bankrupt" = villain with method. David vs. Goliath narrative.

### DepthFirst (ALT — mission framing)
"Trillions of lines of code run our banking, power grid, and healthcare. And it's never been more exposed."

**Why it works:** Stakes are INFRASTRUCTURE — not just business software, but banking, power, healthcare. "Never been more exposed" = urgency without naming a specific attacker.

## TYPE 4: Creative Format Hook (Research-Driven / Viral Format Adaptation)

These adapt recognized viral formats (YouTube, social media) to the product launch.

### DepthFirst — Lie Detector Format
"Interviewer: 'Is there anything you'd like to admit before we begin?' Founder: 'We raised an $80M Series B led by Meritech to secure the world's software before it's too late.' Interviewer: Nods head, gives thumbs up — 'true.'"

**Why it works:** Vanity Fair lie detector format is instantly recognizable. The "admission" IS the announcement. The interviewer confirming "true" = third-party validation of the claim. Playful but the content is dead serious.

### Rokt — Visual Money Format (concept)
"This is what $1.5 billion dollars looks like in single dollar bills and this is how much profit we've added to ecommerce brands like Jones Road Beauty and True Classic."

**Why it works:** Physical visualization of abstract number. Forces the viewer to PICTURE a billion dollars before hearing the claim.

### Meridian — AI Interruption Format
"[Filming a launch video, gets interrupted by a text] 'Hey ChatGPT, I have a date tonight. Can you recommend me a nice place for dinner that says I'm sorry?' [Back to camera] 'Today people are using AI to recommend everything. And if ChatGPT isn't recommending your brand, it's recommending your competitors.'"

**Why it works:** Staged "real life" moment demonstrates the product's thesis (AI recommendations) before introducing the product. The personal/relatable scenario (date night) makes an enterprise problem feel human.

### Viral Format Bank (for research-driven hook development)
1. Jubilee "Odd One Out" — great for comparison products
2. Hot Ones escalation — great for products with increasing capability
3. Vanity Fair Lie Detector — great for bold claims (used: DepthFirst)
4. Mr. Beast challenge — great for measurable outcomes
5. MKBHD Blind Test — great for quality comparison (used: Moda, Icon)
6. 73 Questions (Vogue) — great for fast feature tours
7. Fake Expert — great for AI-vs-human comparison
8. Carpool Karaoke — great for casual founder personality
9. GQ "10 Things" — great for feature showcase
10. Cut "Lineup" — great for selection/matching products

## TYPE 5: Wild Card (Brand-Specific)

These emerge from the specific brand's context — fathom call insights, unique product capabilities, or research discoveries that don't fit other types.

### Durable (HOOK C)
"We raised $20M for this moment. Introducing Durable. Durable is the first AI Business Builder. Durable can create an end-to-end business that can make anyone more than a nine-to-five. Let me show you how it works."

**Why it works:** "For this moment" = cinematic, implies a long journey to this point. Different energy than the other Durable hooks — more reverent, less aggressive.

### Contra — Agentic Commerce
"Creative work and human judgement aren't going anywhere. But the way we work and collaborate is changing forever. Introducing Contra Payments, the world's first payment platform that allows you to sell to AI agents."

**Why it works:** Opens with a PHILOSOPHICAL STATEMENT about the future, then reveals the product is building for that future. "Sell to AI agents" = genuinely novel concept that stops scrolling.

### Arcads (HOOK OPTION 3 — "The Raise" with visual comparison)
"VO: This is an AI ad made on Arcads 1.0, and this is an AI ad made on Arcads 2.0. We just raised a $16M seed round to let anyone create winning ads in minutes. Introducing Arcads 2.0: The first AI ad maker that actually works."

**Why it works:** Version comparison = visual proof of progress. "1.0 vs 2.0" implies massive improvement. "Actually works" acknowledges the viewer's skepticism about AI ads.

## TRANSITION LINES (How Hooks End)

Every hook MUST end with one of these (or a close variant):
- "Let me show you how it works:"
- "Let me show you how powerful [Product] is:"
- "Let us show you how powerful [Product] is:"
- "Let me show you how powerful it is:"

These are the BRIDGE to the body. Non-negotiable.
