# Weapons Library — Intense Copy, Controversy, and Screenshot Moments

Lines that make people react. These are the moments viewers screenshot, quote-tweet, argue about, and share. Every script needs 1-3 of these. The Weapons Agent and Controversy Agent reference this file.

---

## CATEGORY 1: ECONOMY KILLERS (Product kills an entire industry/practice)

"This kills the UGC creator economy entirely." — Arcads
"Arcads lets you create: 30× more ads, 3,000× faster." — Arcads

"We raised $20M dollars to kill the 9-5." — Durable
"The traditional 9–5 job is being eliminated by AI agents." — Durable

"We raised 10 million dollars to kill UGC ads." — Marpipe

"Platforms have stolen $1 BILLION from creatives in the last 12 months." — Contra

**Pattern:** "[Product/AI] kills/eliminates/destroys [entire category/industry/practice]." The claim is EXTREME and specific. Not "disrupts" — KILLS.

**How to create for new brands:** Ask: "What entire category, job title, or practice does this product make obsolete?" State it plainly. Don't soften it.

## CATEGORY 2: SAVAGE COMPARISONS (Old vs. New with brutal specificity)

"On Arcads it costs $10 and takes 5–10 minutes to make a perfect ad. With a real creator it costs $300–$1,000, 3 weeks to get the footage, and the ad still might flop." — Arcads

"Before Parker, a creative team of 3 could make 10 viral ads a week. With Parker, a creative team of 3 can make 100 viral ads a week." — Parker

"Icon also replaces $2K-$30K/month in software costs for just $19/month by combining 10 products into 1." — Icon

"One, you're paying 183% more than your competitors. Two, you have these 7 key coverage gaps that leave your business exposed." — WithCoverage

"On average, Brands see a 4x return on ad spend on old school static and UGC ads but with Marpipe's enriched catalog ads they see an 8x return on ad spend." — Marpipe

"Billion-dollar corporations spend millions optimizing their site, SEO, and online ordering experience to crush local restaurants." — Owner

**Pattern:** Specific old metric → specific new metric. The gap must be DRAMATIC (10x minimum or dollar amounts that make you wince). Include time, cost, AND quality dimensions when possible.

## CATEGORY 3: SUBTLE CONTROVERSY (Provocative but not offensive)

"Or let our human, non-Bombay, designer do it for you." — Marpipe
→ Implies competitors outsource to cheap overseas labor. Provocative reference to offshoring.

"Every other AI design tool creates AI slop." — Moda
→ "AI slop" is a loaded term that dismisses ALL competitors in two words.

"Moda is not a ChatGPT wrapper. We built a Figma-level design canvas first. Then we trained our Agents on typography, layout, and color systems. We taught them taste." — Moda
→ "ChatGPT wrapper" attacks competitors. "We taught them taste" implies competitors' AI has no taste. Three-sentence demolition.

"Other companies are using AI to replace you. We are using AI to make you the business owner." — Durable
→ Positions ALL other AI companies as the enemy of workers. Durable is the exception.

"While other platforms are celebrating eliminating creative jobs with AI, we are dedicated to getting creatives paid billions of dollars by AI." — Contra
→ Same pattern: "they celebrate killing jobs, we celebrate paying you."

"Today all trust is broken. Faces can be faked. Voices can be cloned. And email is dead as inboxes are flooded with AI bots." — Draftboard
→ Paints a terrifying landscape. Every sentence is a weapon.

"In 2 months every sales call will look real. Every voice will sound real. Every email will feel completely personal. But it will all be AI generated." — Draftboard
→ Near-future dystopia as a hook setup.

"Each year, massive corporations quietly bankrupt thousands of small restaurants." — Owner
→ "Quietly bankrupt" is the weapon. Corporations as silent killers.

**Pattern:** The controversy is NEVER random — it's directly tied to the product's positioning. If you're building for creators, attack platforms that exploit creators. If you're building security, paint the threat landscape as terrifying. The controversy makes the product feel necessary.

**More examples from scripts/brands:**
- "You don't want your Filipino VA spending your money on OnlyFans." (Slash — provocative labor reference, implies current outsourcing is untrustworthy)
- "Or let our human, non-Bombay, designer do it for you." (Marpipe — references offshoring, implies competitors use cheap overseas labor)

"Attackers are using AI to break into your systems 10x faster and 10x cheaper than ever before. We are committed to leveling the playing field and protecting companies with AI." — DepthFirst

## CATEGORY 5: TECHNICAL FLEX WEAPONS (Expert lines that make you feel the depth)

"Right now, specialized AI agents running our proprietary security models are scanning every line of your code." — DepthFirst
→ "Proprietary security models" = not off-the-shelf AI. Custom-built.

"Our AI simulates attacks against you, just as a real hacker would." — DepthFirst
→ The AI BECOMES the threat to prove the threat is real.

"Draftboard's agent scans your network and shows you exactly who you should ask for intros — and why." — Draftboard
→ "And why" = the AI doesn't just find paths, it understands relationship dynamics.

"Grader is optimizing every one of your web pages for search, it's generating entirely new visuals like this video-header using Veo 3.1 and it's featuring real customer reviews for you." — Owner
→ Named AI model (Veo 3.1) = technical credibility. Three simultaneous actions in one sentence.

"Icon analyzes 387 Reddit threads in r/running where your potential customers are complaining about caffeine crashes during marathon training." — Icon
→ "387 Reddit threads" + "r/running" + "caffeine crashes during marathon training" = absurd specificity. The AI went DEEP.

"Right now, we're making over 23 insurance carriers compete for your business." — WithCoverage
→ "23 carriers compete FOR you" = flips the power dynamic. You're not shopping — they're competing.

**Pattern:** Name specific technologies, data sources, or numbers. The more granular, the more expert you sound. "Analyzes data" is generic. "Analyzes 387 Reddit threads in r/running" is a weapon.

## CATEGORY 6: CONFIDENCE BETS (Putting money/reputation on the line)

"We're so confident we'll increase your ROAS, that we'll put our money where our mouth is: If you don't see results, I'll donate $10k to a charity of your choice and send you an apology video from my mom." — Marpipe
→ $10k bet + mom involved = absurd, memorable, shareable.

"Mom: 'why are you bringing me into this'" — Marpipe
→ The mom's REACTION is the screenshot moment.

"We also will set the world record for 1 to 100M ARR." — Icon
→ Bold claim about future performance. Either they hit it (legend) or miss it (still got attention).

**Pattern:** The founder/company makes a bet they can't easily back out of. Dollar amount + personal stake + humor = screenshot.

## HOW THE WEAPONS AGENT USES THIS FILE

1. Read the body writer's output
2. For EACH line, ask: "Is there a weapons category this line could be upgraded to?"
3. If a comparison exists → make it more brutal (add specific numbers, name competitors)
4. If a technical line exists → make it more granular (name technologies, add counts)
5. If a mission statement exists → sharpen it with mirror structure ("They X. We Y.")
6. If NO weapon exists in the body → find the 1-2 best places to insert one
7. Keep weapons SUBTLE — they're embedded in the flow, not bolted on

## HOW THE CONTROVERSY AGENT USES THIS FILE

1. Read the weapons-pass body
2. Identify the 1-2 best moments for subtle controversy
3. The controversy must be TIED TO THE PRODUCT'S POSITIONING (not random)
4. Check: "Would this make someone screenshot and share? Would it start a conversation?"
5. Check: "Is this offensive, or just provocative?" — if offensive, soften. If provocative, keep.
6. Insert and ensure it flows naturally with surrounding lines
